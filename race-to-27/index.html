About the Game

"Race to 27" is an exciting subtraction game where players take turns playing cards to reduce a running total. The player who brings the total to 27 or less wins all the cards in the center pile!

How to Play

  1. All cards are dealt equally to players, who keep them face down in a stack.
  2. The dealer starts by playing one card to the center and subtracting its value from 100.
  3. Players take turns playing their top card and subtracting from the new total.
  4. The player who reduces the total to 27 or less wins all cards in the center pile.
  5. Won cards are added to the bottom of the winner's deck.
  6. If a player runs out of cards, they're out of the game.
  7. The game continues until one player has all the cards or only one player remains.

Game Setup

Materials Needed

  • Standard deck of playing cards
  • Paper and pencil for scorekeeping (optional)

Preparation

  • Decide on starting value and number of players
  • Set face card and ace values
  • Shuffle the deck thoroughly
  • Deal all cards equally to players

Game Variations

  • Different Target: Race to 15, 35, or 50 instead of 27
  • Higher Starting Value: Start at 200, 500, or 1000
  • Card Multipliers: Make cards worth 10x their value (2=20, 3=30)
  • Addition Race: Add cards to reach a target sum
  • Multiplication Race: Multiply cards to reach a target product

Educational Benefits

  • Reinforces subtraction skills and mental math
  • Develops strategic thinking about card values
  • Enhances number sense and estimation abilities
  • Encourages quick mental calculations
  • Promotes understanding of number relationships
